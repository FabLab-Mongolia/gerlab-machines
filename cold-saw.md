## What this machine is used for

The cold saw uses a toothed circular blade at low speed to produce controlled cuts in metal with less heat and fewer sparks than an abrasive cutting-off saw.

Common uses:

- Cutting round tube
- Cutting square and rectangular tube
- Cutting angle steel
- Cutting flat bar
- Cutting solid bar within approved capacity
- Preparing accurate stock for welding, machining, and fabrication
- Making straight or approved mitre cuts

---

## Basic Rules

- Use the cold saw only after training or staff permission.
- Use only approved metal and the correct blade.
- Inspect the blade before every use.
- Clamp the workpiece securely in the vise.
- Support long stock on suitable stands.
- Keep the blade guard in place.
- Keep hands outside the cutting zone.
- Do not force the saw head through the material.
- Do not remove offcuts until the blade has stopped.
- Stop if the material moves, the blade binds, or the machine vibrates abnormally.

## Safety Notes

Main risks:

- Contact with the rotating blade
- Sharp blade teeth
- Workpiece movement or ejection
- Broken blade teeth or fragments
- Pinch points at the vise and saw head
- Sharp and hot cut edges
- Coolant contact or spills
- Long stock striking people or objects
- Electrical faults

Safe behavior:

- Wear safety glasses and closed shoes.
- Keep the workpiece firmly clamped on both sides of the cut where practical.
- Stand clear of the blade path and unsupported offcut.
- Lower the blade smoothly and use steady feed pressure.
- Keep the floor dry and clear of coolant.
- Use a brush or tool for chips; do not use bare hands.
- Isolate the machine before blade changes or maintenance.

---

## Personal Protective Equipment

- Safety glasses
- Closed shoes
- Hearing protection when required
- Face shield when the risk assessment requires additional face protection
- Cut-resistant gloves only for handling stationary stock, blades, and sharp offcuts

Do not wear gloves near the rotating blade.

---

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

- Unknown material
- Hardened steel without an approved blade and procedure
- Material containing hidden fasteners
- Cracked, bent, or unstable stock
- Stock too large for the vise or blade capacity
- Material that cannot be clamped securely
- Magnesium or reactive metals without a specific approved procedure
- Wood, masonry, glass, or composites

---

## Material Preparation

Before cutting:

1. Identify the material and profile.
2. Measure and mark the cut.
3. Check the required angle.
4. Remove dirt, oil, or loose scale where necessary.
5. Confirm the material fits within machine capacity.
6. Support long stock at the same height as the vise.
7. Check that the offcut will not fall or trap the blade.

---

## Cutting Workflow

1. Inspect the machine, guard, blade, vise, coolant, and emergency controls.
2. Confirm the blade is suitable for the material and profile.
3. Set the mitre angle and lock it securely.
4. Place and support the stock.
5. Clamp the workpiece firmly.
6. Check hand position and clear the surrounding area.
7. Start the machine and allow the blade to reach stable speed.
8. Lower the saw head smoothly without forcing it.
9. Complete the cut and raise the head.
10. Release the trigger and wait for the blade to stop.
11. Remove the workpiece and offcut safely.
12. Deburr the cut edge.
13. Clean chips and coolant from the work area.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible cause | Safe response |
|---|---|---|
| Cut is not square | Loose vise, wrong angle, blade wear, excessive feed | Stop, reclamp, verify angle, and inspect blade |
| Blade binds | Workpiece movement, wrong blade, excessive pressure | Release trigger, keep hands clear, and wait for full stop |
| Heavy vibration | Loose stock, damaged blade, unsupported material | Stop immediately and inspect setup |
| Rough or burred cut | Worn blade, wrong tooth pitch, poor feed | Check blade and use a staff-approved setup |
| Coolant does not flow | Low level, blockage, pump fault | Stop cutting and report the problem |
| Motor stalls | Excessive feed or unsuitable material | Release pressure, stop, and review the setup |

---

## Maintenance Notes

- Clean chips after every use.
- Wipe and protect unpainted surfaces.
- Check coolant level and condition where fitted.
- Inspect blade teeth, guard, vise, and power cable.
- Keep pivot and vise mechanisms clean.
- Report damaged guards, coolant leaks, unusual noise, or blade runout.
- Blade replacement and electrical work are staff maintenance tasks.
