## What this machine is used for

The horizontal band saw uses a continuous toothed blade to cut metal stock while the saw head feeds downward through the material.

Common uses:

- Cutting round bar and tube
- Cutting square and rectangular tube
- Cutting angle and channel
- Cutting flat bar
- Preparing stock for welding or machining
- Repetitive straight cuts
- Approved mitre cuts

---

## Basic Rules

- Use the saw only after training or staff permission.
- Confirm the material is within machine capacity.
- Select the correct blade and speed for the material.
- Clamp the workpiece firmly in the vise.
- Support long stock correctly.
- Keep guards installed and adjusted close to the work.
- Set hydraulic feed conservatively.
- Keep hands away from the blade and descending saw head.
- Do not remove chips while the blade is moving.
- Stay near the machine until the cut is complete and the blade has stopped.

---

## Safety Notes

Main risks:

- Contact with the moving blade
- Blade breakage or blade fragments
- Pinch and crush points under the saw head
- Workpiece movement or falling stock
- Sharp chips and cut edges
- Coolant spills
- Long-stock handling
- Unexpected automatic head movement

Safe behavior:

- Wear safety glasses and closed shoes.
- Never place any body part beneath the raised saw head.
- Verify the head-lowering control before starting.
- Keep the workpiece flat and fully clamped.
- Use roller stands for long stock.
- Stop if the blade tracks incorrectly, chatters, or makes an unusual sound.
- Isolate the machine before adjusting blade guides, tension, or tracking.

---

## Personal Protective Equipment

- Safety glasses
- Closed shoes
- Hearing protection when required
- Cut-resistant gloves only for stationary stock and sharp offcuts

Do not wear gloves near the moving blade.

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

- Unknown material
- Hardened material without an approved process
- Material with hidden fasteners
- Unstable bundles or loosely grouped pieces
- Bent or cracked stock that cannot sit securely
- Stock exceeding verified machine capacity
- Material too short to clamp securely
- Wood, masonry, glass, or unsafe composites

## Blade Selection Notes

Blade selection depends on:

- Material type and hardness
- Solid or hollow profile
- Wall thickness
- Cross-section size
- Tooth pitch
- Blade width and thickness
- Required finish and cutting speed

At least three blade teeth should normally remain engaged in thin-wall material. Ask GerLab staff before changing blade type or cutting unfamiliar stock.

---

## Cutting Workflow

1. Inspect blade condition, tracking, tension, guards, vise, coolant, and controls.
2. Confirm the material and required cut length.
3. Select the approved blade speed and feed.
4. Set and lock the mitre angle if needed.
5. Place the stock and support long sections.
6. Clamp the workpiece securely.
7. Adjust blade guides close to the work without creating interference.
8. Set hydraulic feed to a conservative starting position.
9. Start the blade and coolant where required.
10. Lower the head under controlled feed.
11. Monitor cutting sound, chip formation, and material stability.
12. Allow the automatic stop to operate, then confirm the blade is stationary.
13. Raise and secure the saw head.
14. Remove and deburr the workpiece.
15. Clean chips and coolant.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible cause | Safe response |
|---|---|---|
| Blade wanders | Low tension, worn blade, excessive feed, loose guides | Stop and have the setup inspected |
| Blade stalls | Feed too high, wrong speed, workpiece movement | Stop and review speed, feed, and clamping |
| Teeth strip | Wrong tooth pitch or excessive feed | Stop and replace blade through staff procedure |
| Excessive vibration | Loose stock, poor support, damaged blade | Stop immediately and correct the setup |
| Crooked cut | Worn blade, guide misalignment, excessive feed | Inspect blade and guides before continuing |
| Coolant failure | Low level, blockage, pump problem | Stop cutting and report the fault |

---

## Maintenance Notes

- Clean chips from the vise, bed, blade area, and coolant tray.
- Check blade condition, tension, tracking, and guide bearings.
- Inspect hydraulic-feed operation and leaks.
- Monitor coolant level and condition.
- Keep guards, limit switches, and automatic stop functional.
- Report unusual vibration, damaged teeth, leaks, or tracking problems.
- Blade installation, alignment, and electrical work are staff tasks.
