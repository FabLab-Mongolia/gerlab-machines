## What this machine is used for

A vinyl cutter uses a small blade to cut vector shapes into thin sheet materials.

It does not print color. It cuts outlines, letters, logos, decals, stickers, labels, masking shapes, and graphic patterns from vinyl or similar sheet materials.

Common uses:

- Stickers
- Decals
- Labels
- Signage
- Window graphics
- Wall graphics
- Laptop stickers
- Car decals
- Logo stickers
- Stencils
- Masking films
- Heat transfer vinyl for textiles, if approved
- Exhibition labels
- Prototype graphics
- GerLab branding and workshop graphics

Vinyl cutting is useful when a project needs clean graphic shapes, signs, labels, or adhesive decoration.

## Basic Rules

- Use the vinyl cutter only after instruction or staff permission.
- Use only approved sheet or roll materials.
- Do not cut unknown materials.
- Check blade depth before cutting.
- Check blade force before cutting.
- Load material straight.
- Make sure pinch rollers are positioned correctly.
- Do a small test cut before the final job.
- Do not pull the material while the machine is cutting.
- Keep hands away from moving parts.
- Do not touch the blade tip.
- Remove finished material carefully.
- Clean the machine area after use.
- Report blade damage, poor cutting, material slipping, strange sound, or machine errors.

## Safety Notes

Vinyl cutters are lower-risk than many machines, but they still include sharp tools and moving parts.

Main risks:

- Sharp blade
- Pinch rollers
- Moving carriage
- Material movement
- Hand cuts during weeding or trimming
- Incorrect material causing poor cutting or damage
- Electrical risk if machine or cable is damaged

Safe behavior:

- Keep fingers away from the blade holder.
- Do not touch the blade tip.
- Keep hands away while the carriage is moving.
- Use tweezers carefully during weeding.
- Use knives or scissors carefully when trimming vinyl.
- Do not place loose objects on the machine.
- Do not force material through the rollers.
- Stop the job if material becomes misaligned.

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

Do not use unknown material.

Restricted or prohibited materials may include:

- Thick plastic sheet
- Hard plastic sheet
- Metal sheet
- Wood
- Cardboard too thick for blade cutting
- Dirty or dusty material
- Wet material
- Material with loose particles
- Material with unknown adhesive
- Material that can damage the blade
- Material wider than machine capacity
- Material that cannot be held correctly by the pinch rollers

Do not use the vinyl cutter as a general cutting machine for random materials.

## File Preparation

Before cutting:

- Use vector artwork.
- Make sure all cut lines are clean paths.
- Remove duplicate lines.
- Remove hidden objects.
- Remove tiny unnecessary details.
- Check artwork size.
- Check units.
- Convert text to outlines if needed.
- Keep enough spacing between objects.
- Add weeding boxes for easier removal.
- Use registration marks only if the workflow requires print-and-cut alignment.
- Mirror heat transfer vinyl before cutting.
- Preview the cut path before sending.

Bad files can waste vinyl and damage the blade.

---

## Cut Line Rules

Good cut lines should be:

- Vector paths
- Closed shapes when possible
- Smooth curves
- Correct scale
- Not duplicated
- Not too small for weeding
- Not too close together
- Not too detailed for the material

Avoid:

- Very tiny text
- Thin fragile shapes
- Excessive detail
- Overlapping paths
- Double cut lines
- Raster images without tracing
- Low-quality auto-traced artwork

## Vinyl Cutting Workflow

### 1. Prepare design

Design the sticker, label, or decal in vector format.

Check:

- Size
- Text outlines
- Cut path
- Spacing
- Weeding difficulty
- Material color
- Final application surface

### 2. Prepare material

Before loading:

- Check material type.
- Check material width.
- Check material backing.
- Check roll direction.
- Check if material is clean and flat.
- Cut the leading edge straight if needed.

### 3. Load material

- Place material under pinch rollers.
- Align material straight.
- Position pinch rollers correctly.
- Make sure material is held firmly.
- Set roll or sheet mode according to the machine workflow.
- Set the origin.

### 4. Test cut

Always perform a test cut before final cutting.

A good test cut should:

- Cut the vinyl layer cleanly.
- Not cut deeply through the backing paper.
- Weed cleanly.
- Keep corners sharp.
- Not tear the vinyl.

### 5. Adjust settings

If the test cut is not good:

- Adjust blade force.
- Check blade depth.
- Check cutting speed.
- Check blade condition.
- Check material loading.

### 6. Cut final job

Send the final job only after a successful test cut.

During cutting:

- Watch material movement.
- Stop if material slips.
- Stop if vinyl lifts or wrinkles.
- Do not pull the material.

### 7. Weed

After cutting:

- Remove unwanted vinyl.
- Use tweezers or weeding tool.
- Work slowly around small details.
- Keep the final design on the backing paper.

### 8. Transfer and apply

For adhesive vinyl:

- Apply transfer tape.
- Burnish transfer tape.
- Lift design from backing.
- Place on clean surface.
- Press evenly.
- Remove transfer tape carefully.

For heat transfer vinyl:

- Mirror before cutting.
- Weed unwanted material.
- Apply according to heat press settings.
- Follow material instructions.

## Common Starting Settings

Vinyl cutter settings depend on material type, blade condition, blade angle, and vinyl thickness.

Always do a test cut first.

| Material | Blade Force | Speed | Notes |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| Standard adhesive vinyl | 80–120 gf | 10–30 cm/s | Good beginner range |
| Thin sticker vinyl | 60–100 gf | 10–30 cm/s | Use lower force first |
| Heat transfer vinyl | 80–140 gf | 10–30 cm/s | Mirror design before cutting |
| Masking film | 80–130 gf | 10–30 cm/s | Test weedability |
| Reflective vinyl | 120–180 gf | 10–20 cm/s | May need higher force and slower speed |
| Thick specialty vinyl | 150–250 gf | 5–20 cm/s | Confirm material is approved |

These are starting points only. Use GerLab-tested settings when available.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Vinyl does not cut fully | Force too low, blade too shallow, dull blade | Increase force, check blade depth, replace blade if needed |
| Backing paper is cut | Force too high, blade too deep | Reduce force, reduce blade exposure |
| Corners lift | Blade offset issue, too much force, dull blade | Check blade, reduce force, slow down |
| Material slips | Pinch rollers wrong, material loaded crooked | Reload material straight, check pinch rollers |
| Cut is misaligned | Material skew, wrong origin, file issue | Reload material, reset origin, check file |
| Small details tear | Design too small, force too high, speed too fast | Enlarge design, reduce force, slow down |
| Weeding is difficult | Cut too shallow or design too detailed | Increase force slightly, simplify design |
| Double cut lines | Duplicate paths in file | Remove duplicate vectors |
| Machine cuts wrong size | Unit or scale issue | Check file size, units, software settings |
| Blade scratches material | Blade too deep, force too high | Reduce blade exposure and force |
| No cut after sending job | Connection/software issue, wrong device selected | Check USB, driver, CutStudio, machine status |
| Rough cut edge | Dull blade, bad material, high speed | Replace blade, slow down, test material |

## Blade Notes

The blade is small but sharp.

Good blade setup:

- Only a very small blade tip should be exposed.
- The blade should rotate freely inside the holder.
- Blade depth should match vinyl thickness.
- Force should be adjusted by test cut, not guessed.
- Use a sharp blade for clean corners.

If the blade is too deep, it can cut the backing paper and damage the cutting strip.

---

## Weeding Notes

Weeding means removing unwanted vinyl after cutting.

Good weeding practice:

- Use a weeding box.
- Start from the corner.
- Pull slowly.
- Use tweezers for small details.
- Weed small internal shapes carefully.
- Keep the backing paper flat.
- Avoid touching adhesive too much.
- Check final design before transfer tape.

---

## Transfer Tape Notes

Transfer tape is used to move adhesive vinyl from backing paper to the final surface.

Basic steps:

1. Place transfer tape over weeded vinyl.
2. Press firmly with scraper or squeegee.
3. Peel backing paper away.
4. Position vinyl on clean surface.
5. Press evenly.
6. Peel transfer tape slowly.

Surface should be:

- Clean
- Dry
- Smooth
- Free from dust, oil, and moisture
