## What this machine is used for

The vertical band saw moves a continuous blade through a fixed worktable while the operator guides the material.

Common uses:

- Straight cuts
- Curved and contour cuts
- Cutting plate and profiles
- Internal cuts using a welded blade loop
- Preparing metal components for fabrication
- Trimming and rough shaping
- Cutting patterns before machining or finishing

---

## Basic Rules

- Use the saw only after training or staff permission.
- Use only approved material and the correct blade.
- Inspect blade condition, tension, tracking, and guides.
- Adjust the upper blade guide close to the workpiece.
- Keep guards installed.
- Keep hands out of the blade path.
- Use push tools for small or narrow pieces.
- Support large material so it remains flat on the table.
- Do not back out of a curved cut while the blade is moving if it may twist the blade.
- Stop if the blade wanders, cracks, chatters, or leaves the wheel.

---

## Safety Notes

Main risks:

- Contact with the moving blade
- Blade breakage
- Hand movement into the cutting line
- Material rotation or sudden movement
- Pinch points at guides and wheels
- Sharp chips and cut edges
- Sparks from the integrated blade grinder
- Electrical and burn hazards at the blade welder

Safe behavior:

- Wear safety glasses and closed shoes.
- Keep fingers to the sides of the blade, never directly in line with it.
- Use relief cuts for tight curves.
- Do not push harder when the blade slows or twists.
- Keep work flat on the table.
- Stop before changing guide height or removing trapped material.
- Isolate power before opening wheel covers or adjusting tracking.
- Use the blade welder and grinder only after separate training.

---

## Personal Protective Equipment

- Safety glasses
- Closed shoes
- Hearing protection when required
- Face shield when grinding or when the task assessment requires it
- Cut-resistant gloves only for handling stationary blades, stock, and sharp offcuts

Do not wear gloves near the moving blade.

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

- Unknown material
- Hardened material without an approved blade and procedure
- Round stock without a fixture that prevents rotation
- Material too small to control safely
- Material that cannot sit flat or be supported
- Material exceeding machine capacity
- Pressurized or sealed components
- Wood, masonry, glass, or hazardous composites

## Blade Selection and Setup

Blade selection depends on:

- Material and hardness
- Material thickness
- Straight or curved cut
- Minimum curve radius
- Tooth pitch
- Blade width
- Required finish

Before cutting:

- Check that the blade is correctly welded and dressed.
- Check blade direction.
- Set correct tension.
- Confirm tracking.
- Adjust side and rear guides.
- Lower the guard and guide assembly near the workpiece.

Blade installation, tracking, and tension adjustment are staff tasks unless the user is specifically trained.

---

## Cutting Workflow

1. Inspect blade, guards, guides, table, controls, and surrounding area.
2. Identify the material and cut shape.
3. Select approved blade speed and blade type.
4. Mark the cut and add relief cuts where needed.
5. Set the table angle if required and lock it.
6. Set the upper guide close to the workpiece.
7. Place the material flat and support large sections.
8. Start the saw and allow the blade to reach stable speed.
9. Feed the material smoothly with hands outside the blade path.
10. Avoid twisting or side-loading the blade.
11. Stop if the blade slows, chatters, or wanders.
12. Complete the cut and move the workpiece clear.
13. Switch off and wait for a complete stop.
14. Remove offcuts and chips with a brush or tool.
15. Deburr the workpiece and clean the area.

---

## Internal Cut and Blade Welding Notes

Internal cuts require the blade to pass through a drilled starting hole and be welded into a continuous loop.

Only trained users may use the built-in blade welder, annealing control, and grinder.

The approved sequence is:

1. Cut the blade square.
2. Clean and align both blade ends.
3. Weld using the correct setting for blade width.
4. Anneal according to the machine procedure.
5. Grind the joint smooth without reducing blade thickness excessively.
6. Inspect the weld before installation.
7. Test the blade at low load.

Discard a blade with a cracked, misaligned, porous, or incomplete weld.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible cause | Safe response |
|---|---|---|
| Blade wanders | Dull blade, low tension, poor guides, excessive feed | Stop and inspect setup |
| Blade breaks | Excessive tension, tight curve, poor weld, side loading | Stop, isolate, and report the break |
| Blade leaves wheel | Tracking fault, low tension, side load | Stop immediately; do not open covers until isolated |
| Teeth strip | Wrong tooth pitch, excessive feed, hard material | Stop and select an approved blade |
| Workpiece chatters | Poor support, wrong speed, excessive feed | Improve support and review settings |
| Weld fails | Poor alignment, welding, annealing, or grinding | Discard or reweld through trained procedure |

---

## Maintenance Notes

- Clean chips from the table, guides, lower wheel area, and floor.
- Inspect blade condition and weld.
- Check guide bearings, blade tension, and tracking.
- Keep wheel tires and covers clean.
- Inspect table lock, guards, speed control, and emergency stop.
- Clean the blade grinder area after use.
- Report unusual noise, cracked blades, damaged guides, or tracking problems.
- Wheel, electrical, and alignment repairs are staff tasks.
