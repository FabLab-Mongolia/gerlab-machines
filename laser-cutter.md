## What this machine is used for

The laser cutter is used for cutting and engraving flat sheet materials. It is useful for prototyping, model making, signage, pattern making, educational tools, art objects, and design experiments.

Common uses:

- Cutting plywood, MDF, acrylic, cardboard, and paper
- Engraving wood, acrylic, leather, paper, and approved surfaces
- Making boxes, signs, models, templates, patterns, and prototypes
- Creating Mongolian pattern-based design samples
- Preparing parts for student and community projects
- Producing educational kits and demonstration models

---

## Basic Rules

- Use the laser cutter only after training or staff permission.
- Never leave the laser cutter unattended while it is running.
- Use only approved materials.
- Do not cut unknown plastics or unknown coated materials.
- Check exhaust / ventilation before starting.
- Watch for flame, heavy smoke, strange smell, or unusual sound.
- Stop the job immediately if something looks unsafe.
- Clean the bed and workspace after use.
- Record useful tested settings after each material test.

---

## Safety Notes

Laser cutting can create fire, smoke, toxic fumes, hot surfaces, and sharp cut edges.

Before using the machine:

- Know where the emergency stop is.
- Know how to pause or stop a job.
- Check that the material is flat and stable.
- Keep the machine lid closed during operation.
- Do not place reflective metal or unknown material inside the machine.
- Ask GerLab staff before testing new materials.

Important safety points:

- Never operate the laser without proper ventilation.
- Never engrave or cut PVC / vinyl.
- Never operate the machine unattended.
- Always monitor vector cutting.
- Use Air Assist during vector cutting.
- Keep the laser clean to reduce fire risk.
- Keep combustible materials and solvents away from the machine.

---

## Air Assist

Air Assist helps reduce flare-ups by directing air across the cutting surface.

Use Air Assist especially when vector cutting materials such as:

- Wood
- Acrylic
- Cardboard
- Paper
- Leather
- Other flammable sheet materials

Before cutting:

- Check that Air Assist is active.
- Check that the material is flat.
- Stay near the machine during the full job.
- Stop immediately if flame grows or does not self-extinguish.

---

## Exhaust / Ventilation

The exhaust system removes smoke, smell, dust, and debris from the laser cabinet.

Before starting:

- Confirm exhaust is turned on.
- Confirm airflow is working.
- Check for unusual smoke buildup.
- Do not operate with leaking or inadequate exhaust.
- Do not use vinyl, plastic, or soft flammable ducting for exhaust connections.

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

Do not use unknown materials.

Materials that may be dangerous must be reviewed before use.

Possible prohibited or restricted materials:

- PVC / vinyl
- Unknown plastic
- Fiberglass
- Carbon fiber
- Reflective metal
- Coated materials with unknown chemicals
- Any material that produces toxic fumes
- Any material that creates uncontrolled flame or heavy smoke

## File Preparation

Before laser cutting or engraving:

- Use vector files for cutting.
- Use raster images for engraving.
- Check scale and units before sending the job.
- Remove duplicate lines.
- Use clean outlines.
- Keep the design inside the working area.
- Test small samples before final cutting.
- Preview the job before running.

Common file formats:

- SVG
- DXF
- PDF
- PNG / JPG for engraving

---

## Raster, Vector, and Combined Mode

### Raster engraving

Raster engraving is used for images, text, photos, logos, filled graphics, and detailed artwork. The laser head moves left and right, engraving the image line by line.

### Vector cutting

Vector cutting follows paths and is used to cut through material or mark outlines.

### Combined mode

Combined mode can engrave and cut in one job. Use this carefully, because hidden thin lines in artwork may become vector cut lines.

---

## Cut Line Setup

Use a consistent method for cut lines.

Recommended:

- Use vector paths for cutting.
- Keep cut lines thin.
- Remove duplicate paths.
- Check the file preview before starting.
- Use color mapping only if the workflow is clear.

For Adobe Illustrator, a thin stroke such as `0.1 pt` or less is commonly used for vector cut paths.

---

## Good Practice Before Running a Job

1. Confirm material is approved.
2. Check file size and scale.
3. Check cut lines and engraving areas.
4. Remove duplicate lines.
5. Check focus.
6. Check exhaust.
7. Check Air Assist.
8. Run a small test if the material is new.
9. Stay near the machine during the whole job.
10. Record useful settings after testing.
11. Clean the machine and work area after use.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible Cause | Fix |
|---|---|---|
| Material does not cut through | Power too low, speed too high, bad focus, material too thick | Refocus, reduce speed, increase power, test again |
| Too much burning | Power too high, speed too low, poor Air Assist | Increase speed, reduce power, check Air Assist |
| Heavy smoke | Material unsuitable, poor ventilation, dirty bed | Stop job, check material, clean bed, check exhaust |
| Engraving too light | Power too low, speed too high, image too bright | Increase contrast, adjust power/speed |
| Engraving too dark | Power too high, speed too low | Reduce power or increase speed |
| Flame appears | Material ignited | Pause/stop immediately and follow safety procedure |
| Cut line engraves instead of cuts | Stroke too thick or process set wrong | Check vector line width and process type |
| File scale is wrong | Wrong units or page setup | Check document size, units, and print settings |
| Duplicate cutting lines | Repeated paths in design file | Remove duplicate vectors before sending |
| Engraving is blurry | Bad focus or low-quality image | Refocus and use higher-quality artwork |
| Edge is too melted | Too much heat | Increase speed, reduce power, test lower frequency if suitable |
| Camera alignment is off | Material not focused or camera needs calibration | Refocus material and check camera calibration |

## Maintenance Notes

Possible maintenance items:

- Clean lens and mirrors only with approved method.
- Clean honeycomb bed.
- Remove small cut pieces and dust.
- Check exhaust.
- Check Air Assist.
- Check that vents are not blocked.
- Report unusual sound, smell, weak cutting, smoke leakage, or machine errors.

## Beginner Workflow

1. Prepare design file.
2. Confirm material is approved.
3. Place material flat on the bed.
4. Focus the machine.
5. Check exhaust and Air Assist.
6. Send file to laser software.
7. Preview engraving and cut paths.
8. Run a small test if needed.
9. Run final job while watching the machine.
10. Remove finished parts after job is complete.
11. Clean bed and workspace.
12. Record useful settings.
