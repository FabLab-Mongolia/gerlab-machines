## What this machine is used for

The cutting-off saw uses a reinforced abrasive wheel to cut metal quickly. It creates more heat, sparks, dust, and burr than a cold saw.

Common uses:

- Cutting round and square tube
- Cutting angle steel
- Cutting flat bar
- Cutting approved solid stock within machine capacity
- Preparing rough stock for welding or further machining
- Making straight or approved mitre cuts

Use the cold saw or band saw instead when surface finish, lower heat, or higher dimensional accuracy is required.

---

## Basic Rules

- Use the machine only after training or staff permission.
- Use only a wheel rated for the machine speed and material.
- Inspect the wheel before each use.
- Never use a cracked, chipped, wet, expired, or unknown wheel.
- Keep the guard correctly installed.
- Clamp the workpiece firmly in the vise.
- Direct sparks toward the approved safe area.
- Remove flammable material from the spark path.
- Let the wheel reach full speed before cutting.
- Do not force or twist the wheel.
- Wait for complete stop before raising guards or removing offcuts.

---

## Safety Notes

Main risks:

- Abrasive-wheel burst
- Contact with the rotating wheel
- Sparks and hot particles
- Fire from the spark stream
- Hot workpiece and sharp burrs
- Workpiece movement or ejection
- Dust and noise
- Pinch points at the vise and saw head

Safe behavior:

- Wear safety glasses and a face shield.
- Use hearing protection.
- Keep everyone out of the spark path.
- Check that guards cover the maximum practical area of the wheel.
- Stand to one side during startup, not directly in line with the wheel.
- Make straight cuts without side-loading the wheel.
- Stop immediately if the wheel vibrates, sounds abnormal, or shows damage.
- Isolate power before wheel changes or maintenance.

---

## Personal Protective Equipment

- Safety glasses
- Face shield over safety glasses
- Hearing protection
- Closed shoes
- Non-flammable workshop clothing suitable for the spark exposure
- Cut-resistant gloves only for handling stationary stock and cooled offcuts

Do not wear gloves near the rotating wheel.

## Approved Materials

Final approval must come from GerLab staff.

Common approved materials may include:

- Mild steel tube and profiles
- Steel angle and flat bar
- Approved ferrous stock within cutting capacity
- Stainless steel only with a wheel specifically rated for it

---

## Prohibited or Restricted Materials

- Aluminum, magnesium, or non-ferrous metal unless the wheel is explicitly rated and staff approve the process
- Unknown material
- Material with hidden fasteners
- Pressurized or sealed tube
- Containers that held flammable substances
- Hardened stock without an approved wheel
- Stock too large or too short to clamp securely
- Wood, plastic, masonry, glass, or composite materials unless both machine and wheel are designed for them

---

## Wheel Inspection

Before installation or use:

- Confirm wheel diameter and bore.
- Confirm the maximum wheel speed is at least the machine spindle speed.
- Confirm the wheel is intended for the material.
- Check for cracks, chips, moisture damage, or contamination.
- Check expiry or use-by information where provided.
- Confirm flanges, blotters, and fasteners are correct.

Wheel changes are staff tasks unless the user is specifically trained and authorized.

---

## Cutting Workflow

1. Inspect wheel, guard, vise, controls, cable, and surrounding area.
2. Remove combustibles from the spark path.
3. Confirm material and wheel compatibility.
4. Set and lock the mitre angle if needed.
5. Place and support the stock.
6. Clamp the workpiece firmly.
7. Confirm the offcut will not jam the wheel.
8. Stand to the side and start the machine.
9. Allow the wheel to reach full speed.
10. Lower the head with steady, controlled pressure.
11. Do not twist or force the wheel.
12. Complete the cut and raise the head.
13. Release the switch and wait for a complete stop.
14. Allow the part to cool before handling.
15. Remove and deburr the workpiece.
16. Clean dust and metal debris safely.

## Common Problems and Fixes

| Problem | Possible cause | Safe response |
|---|---|---|
| Excessive vibration | Damaged wheel, poor mounting, loose stock | Stop immediately and isolate the machine |
| Slow cutting | Worn or unsuitable wheel, excessive stock size | Stop and verify wheel selection |
| Wheel glazing | Wrong wheel or feed technique | Stop and consult trained staff |
| Workpiece moves | Vise not tight or poor support | Stop and reclamp after full wheel stop |
| Heavy burr | Wheel condition, feed, material | Inspect setup and deburr after cooling |
| Sparks reach unsafe area | Poor machine orientation or combustibles nearby | Stop and clear the hazard before continuing |

---

## Maintenance Notes

- Clean abrasive dust and metal debris after use.
- Inspect guard, vise, handle, switch, cable, and wheel flanges.
- Keep ventilation openings clear.
- Check fasteners and machine anchoring.
- Store replacement wheels dry and protected from impact.
- Record wheel changes and damage.
- Electrical repairs and guard modifications are prohibited user tasks.
